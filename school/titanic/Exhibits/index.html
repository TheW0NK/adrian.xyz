Metal of the Titanic

The metal used in the construction of the Titanic had several negative qualities. The steel was found to be brittle at low temperatures, which contributed to the hull fracturing upon impact with the iceberg. Additionally, the rivets used were of substandard quality, which further weakened the ship's structure.

Hull and Bulkheads

The hull and bulkheads of the Titanic had significant design flaws. The bulkheads did not extend high enough, allowing water to spill over into adjacent compartments. This design flaw was a critical factor in the rapid sinking of the ship. Furthermore, the hull's double bottom did not extend up the sides of the ship, which could have provided additional protection against breaches.
